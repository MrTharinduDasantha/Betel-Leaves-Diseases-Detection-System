Common Diseases

Bacterial Leaf Spot Disease

Bacterial Leaf Spot is a serious disease in betel vine caused by bacteria such as Xanthomonas campestris pv. betlicola and Pseudomonas syringae. It leads to significant yield loss if not managed early.

Dried Leaf

Dried Leaf refers to the browning and drying of betel leaves that may not always be caused by a pathogen. It is often due to water stress, environmental factors, or nutrient imbalances rather than a specific infection.

Fungal Brown Spot Disease

Fungal Brown Spot Disease in betel vine is caused by fungal pathogens (such as Phytophthora parasitica and Colletotrichum piperis) and leads to discolored, necrotic lesions on the leaves, reducing both quality and yield.
